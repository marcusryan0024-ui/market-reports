What Happened Today

Futures are still going up on two things: the Iran deal getting closer (oil dropped another 3.8% to $90) and AI spending showing no signs of slowing — Micron hit a $1 trillion market cap yesterday. Nasdaq is up 0.66%, and the Russell 2000 is up 0.78% meaning smaller stocks are now joining the party too. BUT: Zscaler tanked 23% after hours because it cut its cash flow margin guidance — that is dragging down every cybersecurity stock premarket (Palo Alto -5%, CrowdStrike -4%, SentinelOne -5.5%). Tonight is a big earnings night: Marvell, Salesforce, HP, and Snowflake all report after close. How those go will set the tone for AI hardware vs software heading into month-end.

Clear winner vs loser today: AI hardware (chips, memory, servers) is flying while software and cybersecurity are getting hit. Zscaler showed that companies are spending so much on AI compute that it is squeezing their margins — that is bad news for all software/SaaS names. On the good side: SMTC beat earnings and is up 9%, Rocket Lab cleared a $3.5B government satellite program and is up 7.5%, IREN signed a $1.6B Dell Blackwell AI deal. Barclays raised MU target to $1,175 (from $675). Small caps outperforming the Nasdaq today suggests the rally is getting broader. Bernstein conference has JPMorgan and BofA CEOs speaking today — watch for macro comments.
