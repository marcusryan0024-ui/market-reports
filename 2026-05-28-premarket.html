Morning Briefing

Snowflake just had the biggest software earnings beat of the season — up 38% premarket after crushing estimates and raising guidance. That is lifting all cloud and software stocks this morning. But futures are slightly red because oil jumped back up 2.7% to $91 after fresh US-Iran strikes overnight, and tomorrow is the big PCE inflation report (the Fed's favorite gauge). Giant data dump at 8:30am today: Q1 GDP, jobless claims, and durable goods all land at the same time. Costco reports earnings this morning. Dell reports after close — a huge read on AI infrastructure spending. Drone stocks are flying after the Wall Street Journal said Trump is considering taking equity stakes in drone companies.

SNOW's earnings matter for the whole market because investors have been worried that software companies cannot monetize AI. Snowflake proved they can. Salesforce beat earnings massively ($3.88 EPS vs $3.13 est) but fell because its Q2 revenue guide was soft — investors now want clear AI revenue growth, not just cost cuts. Marvell raised long-term AI revenue targets (FY27: $11.5B, FY28: $16.5B) but fell because cloud capex comments were cautious. The pattern: AI hardware still leads, software has to prove itself every quarter now. Oil back above $91 complicates the Fed's job. Tomorrow's PCE is critical — if it comes in hot (est 3.9% YoY), expect pressure on rates-sensitive stocks. Lots of bid-side megacap DP prints into Wednesday's close (MSFT, COST, GOOGL, APP) — institutions positioning ahead of the data stretch.
