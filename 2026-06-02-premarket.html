Morning Briefing

Futures are slightly lower after Monday was another record day. The overnight bombshell: Alphabet just announced an $80 billion equity raise to fund AI infrastructure spending, and Berkshire Hathaway is putting $10 billion into it. That is the biggest single AI capex signal anyone has made — it's bullish for chip and hardware companies but it's also spooking software stocks because investors now wonder if even the biggest companies can keep funding AI forever. Hardware is ripping: HPE blew out earnings (revenue $10.7B vs $9.74B expected, Cloud & AI $7.7B) and is up 26% pre-market. Marvell is up 21% after Jensen Huang called it 'the next trillion-dollar company' at GTC Taipei yesterday. On the other side, software names like NOW, WDAY, SNOW, OKTA, INTU are all down 4-6% as money rotates to semis. DG (Dollar General) beat earnings this morning and is a mover. Big night tonight: PANW, ULTA, GTLB all report after close. AVGO reports tomorrow — that is the big one everyone is watching.

The $80B GOOGL raise is the story that shapes today. Yes, it is bullish for AI hardware suppliers because hyperscalers are spending even more than anyone thought. But it also means GOOGL stock is getting diluted, and it raises a scary question: if even Google needs to raise $80 billion in fresh equity to keep up, how long can this AI buildout last? That uncertainty is why software and SaaS names are getting hit — investors are rotating to the infrastructure layer (NVDA, SMCI, AMAT, LRCX, VRT, MRVL) and away from the application layer for now. Microsoft Build starts today — Satya Nadella's keynote is a major watch for AI platform and Copilot announcements. Computex in Taiwan is still running all week with MU, INTC, WDC presenting. Tencent surged 10% overnight on news it's launching an AI agent through WeChat — lifting Chinese tech (BABA, JD, BYD). JOLTS job openings at 10am sets the tone into ADP Wednesday and Nonfarm Payrolls Friday. Oil down 1.2% to $91 on Iran deal progress. Bond yields falling (10yr 4.43%).
