What Happened Today

Stock futures are slightly higher going into Memorial Day weekend. The US and Iran are talking about a possible deal, which is helping. BUT — China's government cracked down on brokers that let mainland Chinese investors trade US stocks. That sent FUTU crashing -39% and TIGR -37%, and it's dragging down all Chinese stocks listed in the US (BABA, PDD, BIDU, NIO, XPEV all dropping). At 10am ET, two things hit at once: consumer confidence data (already near all-time lows) AND a Fed official speaking — so expect a bumpy open around that time.

Big tech and AI stocks are still running the show. The top 5 mega-cap stocks are outperforming the rest of the market by the biggest gap since 2003 — which means a few big names are carrying the whole index. Software stocks are bouncing back after Workday and Zoom both beat earnings, pushing back against fears that software spending was dying. Oil is still high at ~$100/barrel, which matters because it can push inflation back up and force the Fed to raise rates again. Watch for news on OpenAI potentially filing for an IPO today — that could spark a big AI/tech rally.
