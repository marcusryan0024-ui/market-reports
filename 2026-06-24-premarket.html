Morning Briefing

Futures are modestly green — S&P +0.5%, Nasdaq +0.9%, Russell +0.25% — as investors attempt to stabilize after Tuesday's AI/tech selloff. The Korea KOSPI bounced +3.26% overnight as investors aggressively bought the dip in Samsung (rally to +10% on a potential $59B buyback) and SK Hynix (ADR listing plans, $29B). The overnight semi reversal sets a more constructive tone for US names into today. WEN is the morning's biggest story — up 20%+ pre-market on a new CFO appointment and a massive short squeeze (S3 Partners: ~23% of float short; Trian/Peltz still circling for take-private). The FlowMS edge: WEN appeared on the <3 prints filter yesterday, and the option chain showed heavy OI stacking at the $8/$8.5 strikes well above where the stock traded — filters flagged it before the news broke. Two catalyst sessions define the week: MU earnings AMC tonight and PCE Thursday morning. Everything else is noise until those clear.

Technically this is a bounce, not an all-clear. The S&P 500 remains above key support despite Tuesday's weakness — this is a valuation reset in AI/tech, not the start of a broader bear market. The critical distinction the morning summary makes: traditional macro culprits aren't driving this. The 10-yr yield is at 4.46% (down 3 bps), WTI crude is falling another 2.4% to $71.13 (easing inflation), and Brent is down nearly 2.5%. Iran assured no Strait of Hormuz tolls; Trump's DOJ is investigating oil companies on gas price pace. So rates aren't breaking higher, oil is easing — the selling Tuesday was valuation exhaustion on stretched AI/semi names after an extraordinary run. MU tonight is the clean test: if earnings confirm AI memory demand is real and guidance raises, the dip-buyers win. If MU guides down or warns on oversupply risk from Korean competition, we revisit lows. Everything flows from that print. Thursday's PCE is the macro follow-up — futures pricing 38 bps of tightening in H2 with Warsh's hawkish dot-plot, so a hot PCE re-accelerates the rate fear narrative. A cool PCE gives the Fed room to pivot and is the single biggest bull catalyst of the week.
